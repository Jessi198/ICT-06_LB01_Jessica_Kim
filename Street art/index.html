About This Event

Global Street Art exhibition

What is Street art?

Street art is unofficial and independent visual art created in public locations for public visibility.

What will I see?

You will see the art of the most known Street artists in different forms and learn the story behind. Just come and see!

Why is Street art important?

Street art is a peaceful way to protest against social and political injustice, and often artists make use of this tool to make their voice heard.

Ask Question?

Everything you need to know about our exhibition

Do I need to wear a face covering?
Yes. All guests over the age of 2 are required to wear face coverings over both their nose and mouth for the entirety of their visit.
How many people will be at the museum when I’m there?
The exhibition will be operating at 25% capacity to ensure physical distancing requirements can be met.
Can I take pictures in the exhibiton?
We encourage you to take pictures of the collection and special exhibitions for personal use. Non-flash photography is permitted in all permanent galleries. Signs indicate the few works that are not allowed to be photographed due to a lender requirement.
Is accessible parking available?
Accessible parking is located in several garages near the Art Institute. Please note that they no longer accept oversized vans.
Can I bring my own food? Where can I eat it?
You may bring in your own food to eat at designated eating areas. Limited seating is available at tables spaced for physical distancing. You may also eat outdoors on our front lawn.
Which payment methods do you accept?
We accept the following payment methods: Credit Card or Cash.
Do children require tickets?
Children 6 and under are admitted free, this includes carried children or children in carriers. You do not need to book a ticket. Due to the nature of the exhibition, there must be one adult present for every child under 6 attending the exhibition.
Can I bring my dog to the exhibition?
Dogs or other pets are not permitted in the exhibition, except assistance dogs and guide dogs for the blind. The exhibition welcomes guide dogs fitted with a suitable harness. We ask to register guide dogs prior to your visit.
